# E-Commerece-Dashboard
🛒 E-Commerce Sales Dashboard: Sales Performance & Profitability Insights

An interactive and dynamic Power BI dashboard designed to analyze e-commerce sales performance across different states, product categories, customers, payment methods, and time periods. The dashboard provides valuable insights into revenue generation, profit trends, customer purchasing behavior, and product performance to support data-driven business decisions.

2. Short Description / Purpose

The E-Commerce Sales Dashboard is a comprehensive Power BI report developed to monitor and analyze key sales metrics of an online retail business. It enables users to track sales performance, profitability, customer activity, and product trends through interactive visualizations and filters.

The dashboard is intended for business managers, sales analysts, marketing teams, and decision-makers who need quick access to business insights for improving operational efficiency and increasing profitability.

3. Tech Stack

The dashboard was built using the following tools and technologies:

• 📊 Power BI Desktop – Main platform used for dashboard creation and visualization.

• 📂 Power Query Editor – Used for data cleaning, transformation, and preparation.

• 🧠 DAX (Data Analysis Expressions) – Used to create calculated measures, KPIs, and business logic.

• 🔗 Data Modeling – Relationships established among sales-related tables to support filtering and aggregation.

• 📈 Data Visualization – Bar charts, donut charts, KPI cards, slicers, and column charts used to present insights effectively.

• 📁 File Formats – .pbix for development and .png for dashboard preview.

5. Features / Highlights
• Business Problem

E-commerce businesses generate large volumes of transactional data daily. However, analyzing sales performance manually is time-consuming and often fails to provide meaningful insights.

Business stakeholders frequently need answers to questions such as:

• Which states generate the highest sales?

• Which product categories are most popular?

• Which products contribute the most profit?

• What are the preferred payment methods of customers?

• How does profit vary throughout the year?

• Who are the top customers contributing to revenue?

Without an interactive dashboard, identifying these insights becomes difficult and inefficient.

• Goal of the Dashboard

The primary objectives of the dashboard are:

• To provide a centralized view of business performance.

• To monitor sales, profit, quantity sold, and average order value.

• To identify top-performing products, customers, and regions.

• To analyze customer purchasing patterns and payment preferences.

• To support strategic business decisions through interactive data exploration.

~State-wise Sales Analysis (Bar Chart)

Displays the total sales amount generated by different states.

Key Insight:

• Maharashtra contributes the highest sales revenue.

• Madhya Pradesh follows as the second-highest performing state.

This visualization helps identify strong and weak geographical markets.

~Category-wise Quantity Distribution (Donut Chart)

Shows the percentage contribution of each product category to total quantity sold.

Key Insight:

• Clothing contributes approximately 63% of total sales quantity.

• Electronics contributes 21%.

• Furniture contributes 17%.

This helps understand customer demand across product categories.

~Monthly Profit Analysis (Column Chart)

Displays monthly profit performance throughout the year.

Key Insight:

• Several months generate strong profits, while a few months show lower profitability.

• Seasonal trends can be identified for better business planning.

This visualization supports forecasting and strategic planning.

~Profit by Sub-Category (Horizontal Bar Chart)

Displays profit generated by various product sub-categories.

Key Insight:

• Printers generate the highest profit.

• Bookcases rank second in profitability.

• Sarees, Accessories, and Tables also contribute significantly.

This visualization helps identify the most profitable products.

Interactive Filters

The dashboard includes:

• Quarter Filter (Q1, Q2, Q3, Q4)

• Category Filter

These filters allow users to dynamically analyze data for specific periods and product categories.
